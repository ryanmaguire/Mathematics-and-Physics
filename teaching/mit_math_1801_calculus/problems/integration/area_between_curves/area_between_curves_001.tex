Let $R$ be the finite region that is trapped between the
two curves $f(x)=x^{3}$ and $g(x)=x^{1/3}$ and that lies
in the first quadrant of the $xy$ plane
(that is, $x\ge{0}$ and $y\ge{0}$).
\par\hfill\par
Compute the area of $R$.

\edXabox{
    type="numerical"
    size="50"
    expect="0.5"
    feqin="1"
}

\answer{
    On the interval $[0,\,1]$ we have $x^{3}\leq{x}^{1/3}$.
    We can compute the area by integrating the difference.
    \[
        A=\int_{0}^{1}
            \left(x^{1/3}-x^{3}\right)\,\textrm{d}x
        =\int_{0}^{1}x^{1/3}\,\textrm{d}x
            -\int_{0}^{1}x^{3}\,\textrm{d}x
    \]
    This can be computed using the power rule for integrals. We have:
    \[
        \int_{0}^{1}x^{1/3}\,\textrm{d}x-\int_{0}^{1}x^{3}\,\textrm{d}x
        =\frac{3}{4}-\frac{1}{4}
        =\frac{1}{2}
    \]
}
